About me

I’m a quantitative ecologist at Aarhus University interested in macroecological patterns. I am currently part of the Plant Evolution and Biodiversity group where I explore large-scale biodiversity patterns of plants by integrating geographic, phylogenetic and environmental data, with a special focus on Tropical Rainforest Hyperdiversity.

I have worked on deep time paleontological and neontological topics, including extinction risk factors in invertebrates and amphibians, geographic range size patterns, diversity, and the effect of climate on extinction risk factors.
